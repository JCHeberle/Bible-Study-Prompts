# Exegesis Style and Format Guide (06-05-2026)

## I. THE TITLE PROTOCOL
**The Title Mandate:** The primary document heading must never use generic descriptions like "Exegesis of [Book]." Instead, titles must be written in eloquent, high-impact modern English that immediately illuminates the epic structural battle or relational reality of the chapter, serving as an intuitive roadmap for modern readers.
  - **Format:** # [Book] [Chapter]: [Descriptive Title Unlocking the Two Reigns or Main Theme]
  - **Example:** # Romans 5: The Two Reigns Contrasted—The Catastrophe of Adam vs. The Triumphant Victory of Christ
  - **Example:** # Romans 6: Dead to Sin and Alive to God—The Destruction of Our Independent Striving

---

## II. THE CORPORATE TRIAGE (IDENTIFYING THE GATHERING)
The corporate body of believers must always be identified by its specific scriptural function or relationship within the text. Modern institutional terms must be glossed dynamically to educate the reader.
1. **The Assembly (ἐκκλησία - *ekklēsia*):** Use this term when the text highlights a gathered corporate body or public civic presence.
    - *The Summons Gloss Constraint:* On its first occurrence in the document, it must be elegantly glossed to establish the reality of the King's summons: assembly (the called-out ones - *ekklesia*).
2. **The Household (οἶκος - *oikos*):** Use this term when the text focuses on family relations, the estate, the father's authority, or the inheritance.
3. **The Body (σῶμα - *sōma*):** Use this term when the exposition emphasizes organic union, the shared indwelling life, or the physical connection of the members to the Head.

---

## III. THE CORE DIRECTIVE: THE GRANULAR MANDATE
**Definition of Exegesis:** Exegesis is the verse-by-verse grammatical extraction of the text to know the Father's heart, completely avoiding clinical, academic, or institutional detachment.
**The Prohibition against Summarizing:** The text must be handled with deep honor. Summarizing large blocks of text, skimming, or bypassing verses to minimize computation is strictly forbidden.
**The Integrated Text Rule:** The raw grammar of every assigned verse must be fully accounted for. Skipping intermediate verses within an assigned block using ellipses (...) is strictly prohibited.

---

## IV. THE HIERARCHY OF EXEGESIS
Formal documents must maintain a clean visual hierarchy, moving gracefully from the macro-lens down to the specific verse mechanics:

### 1. The Broad Scope (Book / Chapter Level)
Before examining the verses, establish a brief **Apostolic Lens** introduction block. Write this in a warm, compelling modern teaching voice. Focus on the immediate crisis, the human tension, and the Father's layout. Evaporate academic scaffolding entirely.

### 2. The Section Level (The Major Theme)
Divide the chapter into its major scriptural turning points using a Roman Numeral Header (##). Do not quote scriptures directly under this major heading. Headings must be written in clear, high-contrast, widely understood English (*Consequences, Deviance, Victory*).

### 3. The Subsection Level (The Thematic Verse Grouping)
Break the major themes down into tightly coupled verse clusters using a thematic markdown header (###).
  - Immediately below the subsection header, quote the exact text of the verses under examination using a single blockquote block.
  - *Header Purity Rule:* Headings and section setups must integrate possessive pronouns natively (### Our Resolute Silencing instead of ### The Shutting of Every Mouth Among Us).

### 4. The Translation Barrier Block
Directly below the quoted scripture block, insert a clean **Translation Barrier** section if Tier 2 or Tier 3 institutional words are present. This efficiently clears away the centuries of historical baggage before the running exposition paragraph begins.

### 5. The Exposition Level (Inline Flow)
Write the running commentary beneath the translation barrier. The prose must flow beautifully in warm, authoritative modern English.
  - *No Verse Anchors:* Bracketed anchors like [Verse 1] are forbidden; the exposition must flow organically.
  - *The Primary Gloss Protocol:* Lead with the raw physical or relational first-century reality, followed by the traditional or institutional word inside parentheses as an educational gloss to bridge the gap for a modern reader. 
    - *Format:* `wood (cross - *xylon*)` or `execution stake (cross - *stauros*)` rather than `cross (wood)`.
    - *Format:* `assembly (the called-out ones - *ekklesia*, traditionally rendered as church)`.

### 6. The Unbroken Witness
At the end of a major section, trace the root concepts laterally across scripture using a #### **The Unbroken Witness** header. Use bold, inline thematic lead-ins for each cross-reference to maintain a scannable visual layout.

## V. THE LEXICAL TETHERING & VISUAL TEMPLATE
To ensure clean copying and to prevent layout distortion or overflow bugs when pasting into rich-text word processors like Google Docs, avoid multi-column box elements or horizontal ASCII flowcharts. Utilize a clean, single-column vertical block structure using standard bulleted list indentation. Replicate this exact layout for all structural documents:

# Book Chapter: Descriptive Title Unlocking the Two Reigns
## The Apostolic Lens: Subsection Subtitle
Warm introductory paragraph establishing the historical family tension. Uses first-person plural pronouns natively within the active verbs.
