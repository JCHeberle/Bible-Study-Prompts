# Bible-Study-Prompts
Bible Study Prompts Studying the Biblical Texts through a thought-for-thought apostolic 50-95 AD lens.

BIBLE STUDY PROMPTS: USER MANUAL AND FRAMEWORK GUIDE
====================================================

Welcome to the apostolic, thought-for-thought Bible study framework. This guide explains the core philosophy behind the prompt, why certain words and concepts are excluded, and how to get the most out of your AI-assisted study.

THE CORE PHILOSOPHY: WHY THE EXCLUSIONS EXIST
---------------------------------------------

The banned word stems and rejected theological terms in this framework exist for one central reason: to protect the core axiom that the Scriptures are the inspired Word of God and must therefore be perfectly consistent. Every passage must logically "AND" with every other passage without any alteration to the plain meaning of the original languages. Many common theological terms were developed in later centuries specifically to resolve apparent tensions by subtly redefining what the text actually says. The AI naturally tends to drift toward these majority opinions and post-apostolic systems—the broad path Jesus warned leads most people astray. 

By enforcing these exclusions, the prompt blocks those rabbit trails entirely and keeps every analysis strictly within the 1st-century apostolic lens, where the New Testament understanding always governs the Old, and the narrow way is honored above popular consensus. The exclusions are defensive tools that ensure the output remains pure Scripture interpreting Scripture, without modern theological packaging.


HOW THE "SEMANTIC FIREWALL" PROTECTS YOUR STUDY
-----------------------------------------------

If you are using this framework, you might wonder why it restricts the AI from using certain common theological phrases. Think of these exclusions not as censorship, but as guardrails on a steep mountain highway. They keep the AI from driving off the cliff into post-biblical traditions. Here is why this approach works:

1. The Principle of Unaltered Consistency
Our founding presupposition is that God's Word does not contradict itself. Therefore, we do not need later theological "shortcuts" to make verses fit together. This framework forces the AI to do the hard work of harmonizing the text as it was originally written, ensuring that every analysis is an inevitable consequence of the text itself, rather than a forced doctrinal opinion.

2. Counteracting AI "Drift"
Large Language Models are trained on massive amounts of data, which means they naturally default to the "majority report." If millions of theological articles use a specific 4th-century term to explain a 1st-century verse, the AI will naturally drift toward that popular consensus. Our framework acts as a deliberate counter-measure to this drift, forcing the AI off the "broad path" of internet consensus and back onto the "narrow way" of the apostolic text.

3. Avoiding Historical Shortcuts
Throughout history, well-meaning scholars have invented complex vocabulary to explain away apparent tensions in Scripture. While popular, these terms often subtly redefine the original Greek (α) and Hebrew (א) texts. By omitting these modern terms, we prevent the AI from importing centuries of external baggage into your study session.


NAVIGATING NUANCES AND EDGE CASES
---------------------------------

As you use this prompt, especially in small groups or Discord study sessions, you might encounter a few edge cases. Here is how to navigate them:

* "This feels restrictive!" 
It is completely normal to feel a bit of friction when you first use this framework. However, these exclusions are purely defensive tools. They are not designed to ban ideas, but to protect the 1st-century lens. Think of it like a water filter: it doesn't restrict your water; it simply removes the sediment so you can drink it pure.

* "Does this framework argue for a specific denomination?"
No. This framework never argues for or against any specific modern theological view. It simply enforces the axiom that the text must agree with itself. Users who prefer to view Scripture through the lens of modern denominational frameworks will naturally self-select out of using this prompt—and that is exactly what the narrow-way principle intends.

* "Will this work on my preferred AI model?"
Yes! Whether you are using Grok, Gemini, Claude, or a local open-source model, the logic holds up. The framework relies on foundational prompting logic, not model-specific quirks, so you will get consistent, apostolic-lens results everywhere.


BEST PRACTICES FOR USAGE
------------------------

* Share Freely: You can confidently share this prompt in forums, study groups, or with students. Because the framework is strictly governed by Scripture interpreting Scripture, it provides a highly rigorous, objective baseline for group discussion.

* Reviewing Outputs: When reviewing the AI's answers, always check them against this core manual. Ask yourself: Did the AI successfully logically "AND" the passages together, or did it try to sneak in a modern theological shortcut?
